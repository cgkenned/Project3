THE BREAKDOWN

As of June 2016, a third of New York's Department of Youth and Community Development (DYCD) focuses on legal assistance and services. The next largest section goes toward domestic violence among immigrant families. Services for immigrant families are designed to help immigrant parents of English Language Learners (ELL) in grades 6 through 12 understand the requirements of the New York City school system and take an active role in their children's education. General immigrant/refugee assistance can involve accessing government benefits, job applications, healthcare, and civics classes in preparation for citizenship. NDA immigrants live in neighborhood development areas, and the DYCD distributes grant money to these neighborhood pockets to aid with social services.

THE BREAKDOWN

New York City’s Department of Youth and Community Development (DYCD) offers five types of services to immigrant and refugees in different NYC boroughs:

  • Legal assistance and services - for citizenship, immigration status, etc.
  • Domestic Violence - often, immigrant families will not report domestic violence abuse due to fears of being deported; these services aim to provide relief for families who may otherwise not report.
  • Immigrant Family Services - designed to help immigrant parents of English Language Learners (ELL) in grades 6 through 12 understand the requirements of the New York City school system and take an active role in their children's education.
  • General immigrant/refugee assistance - can involve accessing government benefits, job applications, healthcare, and civics classes in preparation for citizenship.
  • NDA Services - NDA immigrants live in neighborhood development areas, and the DYCD distributes grant money to these neighborhood pockets to aid with social services.

GROWING TRENDS

A 2015 American Immigrantion Council study reported that the Latino share of New York’s population grew from 12.3% in 1990, to 15.1% in 2000, to 18.4% (or 3,606,708 people) in 2013. The Asian share of the population grew from 3.8% in 1990, to 5.5% in 2000, to 7.9% (or 1,554,980 people) in 2013.

As seen in the above graph, the percentage of Asian and Latino immigrants in New York City has steadily increased from 1990 to 2013, and they now make up a signficant portion of NYC's electorate.
